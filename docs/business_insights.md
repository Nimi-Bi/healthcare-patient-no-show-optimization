Healthcare No-Show Optimization Project
Business Insights


Query 1: Overall Patient No-Show Rate

Business Question

What percentage of scheduled appointments resulted in patient no-shows?

Result

Total Appointments: 9,725
Total No-Shows: 1,888
No-Show Rate: 19.41%

Business Insight

Nearly 1 in 5 scheduled appointments resulted in a no-show, indicating a significant operational and financial challenge for the healthcare organization.


Query 2: Revenue Loss by Department

Business Question

Which department experiences the highest financial loss due to patient no-shows?

Result

Department	Revenue Loss ($)
Cardiology	116,358
Orthopedics	89,918
Dermatology	46,192
General Medicine	40,667
Pediatrics	31,954

Business Insight

Cardiology generated the highest revenue loss from missed appointments, suggesting that no-show reduction initiatives should be prioritized within this department.


Query 3: SMS Reminder Effectiveness

Business Question

Do SMS reminders reduce patient no-show rates?

Result

SMS Reminder	No-Show Rate
Sent	14.97%
Not Sent	30.11%

Business Insight

Patients who received SMS reminders were significantly more likely to attend their appointments. SMS reminders reduced no-show rates by approximately 50%.


Query 4: Waiting Time Impact

Business Question

How does appointment waiting time affect patient attendance?

Result

Waiting Time	No-Show Rate
0–7 Days	12.90%
8–14 Days	15.56%
15–30 Days	19.16%
31+ Days	21.99%

Business Insight

No-show rates increased steadily as waiting times increased. Patients waiting more than 30 days had the highest risk of missing appointments.


Query 5: No-Show Rate by Day of Week

Business Question

Which days of the week experience the highest patient no-show rates?

Result

Day	No-Show Rate
Thursday	20.89%
Sunday	20.68%
Saturday	19.91%
Wednesday	18.91%
Monday	18.82%
Tuesday	18.52%
Friday	18.25%

Business Insight

No-show rates were relatively consistent across the week. Thursday recorded the highest rate, although day-of-week differences were modest compared with the impact of SMS reminders and waiting times.


Executive Summary

The analysis identified a 19.41% overall patient no-show rate, resulting in an estimated revenue loss of $325,089. Cardiology experienced the highest financial impact, while SMS reminders demonstrated a substantial reduction in missed appointments. Longer waiting times were associated with higher no-show rates, suggesting that scheduling optimization and reminder programs could significantly improve operational efficiency and patient attendance.
